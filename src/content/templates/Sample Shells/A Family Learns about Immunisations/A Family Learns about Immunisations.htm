Basic Text & Picture

a girl and a younger boy play in front of a house on a sunny, clear day. Their mother is in the doorway, talking to them.

Nelli and Mattu were playing in the yard.

“Nelli!” called Mama. “Take your brother and go to the water tap for more water. I need to wash the baby.”

Basic Text & Picture

The two children carry a bucket as they walk toward a water pump. Near the pump stand a group of women talking. One looks like a woman from town.

Nelli and her brother got containers and walked to the water tap. 

They saw a stranger at the tap. She was talking to people as they got water.

Basic Text & Picture

The children stand at the pump with the bucket underneath, filling with water. The town woman faces them to talk to them.

Nelli began to fill the containers with water. Just then the lady said to her, “Hello there, I'm Nurse Kerol from the clinic. What are your names?”

Basic Text & Picture

the children and the town woman talk to each other at the water pump

Nelli replied, “I am Nelli, and this is Mattu. We are getting water for my mother, so she can give our baby sister a bath.”

“You are such a good helpers. Can I help you carry the water home? I would like to talk to your mother.”

Basic Text & Picture

The girl and the woman walk with the full bucket between them. The boy chases bugs behind them. They are near to the children's house.

They all walked back to the house together. When they were almost home, Nelli called out, 'Mama, Mama! There is someone here to see you.”

Basic Text & Picture

Mama, holding the baby, stands in the doorway. She recognizes the woman and greets her.

Mama came to the door, carrying the baby.

“Hello! Nelli, I know your new friend. She grew up in the village next to ours. Welcome to our house, Kerol."

Basic Text & Picture

The women sit in the house (on mats? at a table?) drinking tea. The baby is with her mother. The girl and the boy are in the back ground.

They drank tea and talked. Kerol said, “As a nurse, I worry about the children in our villages. They need to have their baby shots so they don't get the childhood sicknesses that can kill them.”

Mama replied. “But when they get sick we can take them to the clinic for a shot. They will cry otherwise.”

Basic Text & Picture

women talking and drinking tea with kids watching

‘“Oh these are not shots that make sick children better,” replied Kerol. “These are shots to give them protection from future sicknesses.”

“How can a shot give protection for the future? I thought shots gave you strong medicine to kill the sickness you have already?”

Basic Text & Picture

a picture of a healthy garden with a strong fence around it

“Well,” Kerol said, “the shot works like a fence. You make a strong fence around your garden to protect the things you will grow in it. And now you have lots of healthy food growing there - sweet potatoes, beans, pumpkins and other great plants.”

Basic Text & Picture

picture of pigs ruining a garden

Mama agreed. “Yes, we build a strong fence to protect our plants so they can grow well. Without a good fence, pigs and other animals will ruin our garden.”

“Well, just like the fence, the immunisations work to protect the children from attacks by certain illnesses,” Kerol replied.

Basic Text & Picture

"Without immunisations, your children don’t have a lot of protection. They can get serious sicknesses like measles, TB, polio and tetanus."

picture of kid with lock jaw

Basic Text & Picture

“You mean I can stop my kids from getting these bad illnesses?” said Mama. “When can they have these shots?” 

Kerol replied, “We will come to the village in three weeks to give the children their shots. But you can also come to the clinic to get the immunisations.”

“Hmm, I think I'll bring them to the clinic tomorrow,” Mama said. “They need that fence of protection now!”
